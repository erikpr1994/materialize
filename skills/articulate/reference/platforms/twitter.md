X is a public timeline ranked by per-post engagement velocity; readers skim fast and most of your reach is to people who don't follow you yet.

- **Make the first tweet stand fully alone** — only ~1-2 lines (~100-200 chars) show before "show more", and the ranker scores the opener's early engagement. Convey the payoff above the fold; no "a thread:" throat-clearing.
- **Lead with a concrete result, number, or surprising claim** — "I cut our build from 9min to 40s" stops the scroll where "5 lessons I learned" doesn't. Specific claims are quotable and screenshot-able, which drives reshares.
- **Optimize for replies and bookmarks, not likes** — replies are the heaviest signal and bookmarks a strong save signal; likes are weighted low. Useful posts earn saves, real questions earn replies, and both compound into reach.
- **Reply to your own early replies in the first 30-60 min** — early velocity decides whether a post escapes your network (roughly half of distribution is out-of-network). Author replies count as engagement and author-led threads get boosted.
- **One idea per tweet; thread only as long as the idea demands** — each tweet must earn the tap to the next, and padding raises drop-off where completion and dwell are rewarded. Threads land best at ~4-8 tweets; a complete standalone tweet beats a forced thread. Hard cap 280 chars (Premium goes ~25k, but length buys no reach).
- **Put external links in a reply or self-quote, not the main tweet** — X historically throttled outbound links; the penalty was reportedly removed Oct 2025 but 2026 status is contested. The reply-link costs nothing and hedges what you can't verify.
- **Post code as a clean screenshot; prefer native media** — X strips formatting from raw code pastes, but an image preserves it and reshares well. Native media (short video, one clear diagram) renders in-feed, raises dwell, and watched video gets an explicit boost.
- **Treat it as plain text** — no markdown renders (no bold, headers, or backticks); URLs and @/# tokens auto-linkify. Structure with line breaks, blank lines, and numbering; emoji-as-bullets sparingly. Hashtags no longer aid discovery and read as spam to devs.
- **Write for the bookmark-and-send test** — would a peer save this or forward it to a teammate? Saves, qualified replies, and follow-converting profile visits compound into reach and real opportunities; posts engineered for cheap likes draw low-value engagement and negative signals.

## For a project post
- Lead with what it does and the one number that proves it — latency, lines deleted, time saved — not "excited to announce".
- Drop a screenshot or short demo clip in the main post; it renders in-feed and holds attention longer than a link.
- Put the repo or write-up link in the first reply.
- Make the opener understandable to someone who's never seen the project, then let the thread go deep for those who tap in.

## Skip
- **Hashtags on every post** — they no longer drive discovery and read as spam to a technical audience.
- **Padding posts longer because Premium allows it** — dwell and completion buy reach, not length.
- **Forcing everything into a thread** — many ideas land harder as one complete tweet; forced threads raise drop-off.
- **Chasing quoted viral multipliers (27x, 1,700%)** — marketing-blog inventions with no traceable source.
- **Buying Premium for "10x reach"** — the boost amplifies engagement you already earned; it won't rescue a weak post.
- **Hunting the one magic posting time** — early velocity from your real network beats clock time, especially for an international audience.
- **Treating followers or likes as the scoreboard** — half of reach is out-of-network velocity; likes are vanity.
- **Engagement bait (RT-if, follow-for-follow)** — draws low-value engagement and mutes/blocks that suppress future reach.
- **Posting on cadence alone** — frequency without per-post velocity and saves is just broadcasting.
